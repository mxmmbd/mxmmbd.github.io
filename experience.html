HSLU · Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) AI & Robotics Researcher Sep – Dec 2024
HSLU / SBB — AI & Robotics Researcher
Real-time rail detection system for an autonomous maintenance robot. 97.8% accuracy at 30 FPS. Sensor hardware designed and iterated across three cycles. Presented at Swiss Robotics Day 2024, Basel.
+
Overview

Research position at the AI & Robotics Lab, Lucerne University of Applied Sciences (HSLU), in collaboration with Swiss Federal Railways (SBB). Supervised by Prof. Björn Jensen. The project focused on a computer vision system for an autonomous rail-maintenance robot performing vegetation management without entering active tracks.

The ROS 2-based real-time detection pipeline achieved 97% validation accuracy and 80% external accuracy in one framing, and 97.8% rail detection accuracy at 30 FPS in deployment. Results were presented at Swiss Robotics Day 2024, Basel.

In parallel, sensor mounting hardware was designed and iterated for LiDAR, Intel RealSense D435, and a 360° camera. Original PLA mounts cracked under vibration; resolved through geometry redesign, PETG material, and damping isolation across three iterations. A UR3e mounting assembly integrating Raspberry Pi and RealSense was prototyped, solving cable routing and alignment across the full arm range of motion.

Technical Highlights
  • Rail detection: 97% validation / 80% external accuracy; 97.8% at 30 FPS in deployment
  • ROS 2-based inference pipeline with real-time RViz2 visualization
  • Sensor mounts: three iterations — PLA → geometry + PETG + damping isolation
  • UR3e mounting assembly: Raspberry Pi + RealSense D435 integration; cable routing and alignment solved across full arm motion
  • Presented at Swiss Robotics Day 2024, Basel
  • CAD in Fusion 360
