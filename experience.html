+
Overview

Research at the AI & Robotics Lab, HSLU, in collaboration with Swiss Federal Railways (SBB). Supervised by Prof. Björn Jensen. Built a real-time rail detection system for an autonomous rail-maintenance robot performing vegetation management without entering active tracks.

ROS 2-based pipeline achieved 97% validation accuracy / 80% external accuracy in one framing, and 97.8% at 30 FPS in deployment. Results presented at Swiss Robotics Day 2024, Basel. Sensor mounting hardware (LiDAR, Intel RealSense D435, 360° camera) iterated across three design cycles — PLA → geometry redesign + PETG + damping isolation. Designed and prototyped UR3e mounting assembly with Raspberry Pi and RealSense, solving cable routing and alignment across full arm range of motion.
