F1TENTH, ForzaETH: Autonomous Racing
Steering system design, embedded control, and structural reliability for a 1:10 autonomous race car. Feedback error under 5%, response time ~0.2 s. Integrated in ForzaETH ROS autonomy stack for ICRA 2024.
+
Overview

Bachelor thesis at ETH Zurich (PBL Lab, Dept. of Electrical Engineering) with ForzaETH. Supervised by Prof. John Lygeros and Prof. Michele Magno. Focused on steering system design, embedded control, and structural reliability for a 1:10 autonomous race car under competitive conditions.

The original plexiglass sensor mounting plate fractured under collisions, causing LiDAR misalignment and ~1.5 h replacement time per incident. A redesigned structural solution (Siemens NX) eliminated the failure mode. Servo-actuated steering validated with feedback error under 5% and response time ~0.2 s. Embedded control via STM32, Arduino Pico, and custom PCB within the ROS autonomy stack. Contributions tied to ForzaETH's platform for ICRA 2024.

Technical Highlights
  • Plexiglass mount redesigned → LiDAR stability restored; ~1.5 h replacement cycles eliminated
  • Servo steering: feedback error <5%, response time ~0.2 s
  • Custom test fixtures; static + dynamic load testing: slalom, figure-eight, circular trajectories
  • All data recorded in ROS bags for systematic validation
  • STM32, Arduino Pico, custom PCB — real-time steering feedback in ROS stack
  • CAD in Siemens NX

Columbia University · ROAM LabResearch2026
MiniBEE — Manipulator Kinematic Optimization
Optimizing the MiniBEE manipulator kinematic chain across 6, 7, and 8 DOF configurations to minimize singularities and self-collisions while achieving full coverage of a 20 × 20 × 20 cm workspace.
+
Overview

Research at the ROAM Lab, Columbia University. MiniBEE is a compact robotic manipulator. The core question: which DOF configuration (6, 7, or 8) achieves fewest singularities and self-collisions while maintaining full coverage of a 20 × 20 × 20 cm cubic workspace. Involves URDF modeling, MoveIt configuration in ROS 2, and quantitative workspace and manipulability evaluation across candidate configurations.

Technical Highlights
  • Evaluating 6, 7, and 8 DOF kinematic chains for singularity and self-collision minimization
  • Target: full coverage of 20 × 20 × 20 cm workspace
  • URDF models + MoveIt configured in ROS 2
  • Workspace coverage and manipulability metrics for quantitative comparison
