Crawler Robot
8-legged Klann-linkage crawler designed, fabricated, and programmed from scratch. Crab walking, IMU-driven body reorientation, and RL-trained locomotion transferred to hardware.
+
Overview

Developed in Robotic Studio at Columbia University (Creative Machines Lab, Prof. Hod Lipson). Working from a servo kit and open design brief, the project produced a spider-inspired 8-legged crawling robot using a Klann linkage mechanism. Two actuated arms drive the full 8-legged structure. Designed in Fusion 360, fabricated on a Bambu Lab printer with a 0.4 mm nozzle.

Two full redesign cycles completed: the first addressed material distribution and joint tolerances; the second resolved mechanical binding from unavailable ideal fastener sizes. Final joint tolerances: 0.4–0.8 mm. Electronics soldered and integrated throughout.

Technical Highlights
  • Klann linkage 8-legged mechanism driven by two servo arms; designed in Fusion 360
  • Two redesign cycles: material reduction + tolerance improvement; fastener binding resolution
  • Platform: 6 servos, Raspberry Pi, IMU; electronics soldered on-board
  • Crab walking gait and body reorientation via IMU feedback
  • Gait optimization: random search, hill climber, parallel mutation hill climber
  • MuJoCo simulation for gait validation; PPO via Gymnasium + stable-baselines3 for sim-to-real

Safe Path Generation — Franka Panda Arm
Real-time collision avoidance for a 7-DOF Franka Emika Panda. IK via-point planner vs. Jacobian-based velocity controller. Jacobian approach achieved higher success rates and smoother trajectories.
+
Overview

Course project for Introduction to Robotics (RoAR Lab, Prof. Sunil K. Agrawal). Focused on enabling a 7-DOF Franka Emika Panda arm to navigate safely in constrained, cluttered workspaces motivated by laboratory chemical-handling scenarios.

Two control architectures compared in Genesis physics engine simulations with stochastic obstacles: an IK solver with geometric via-point, and a Jacobian-based velocity controller with real-time link-wise avoidance. The Jacobian approach consistently achieved higher success rates, smoother trajectories, and lower sensitivity to obstacle placement.

Technical Highlights
  • Robot modeled using Modified Denavit-Hartenberg (MDH) convention
  • IK: via-point offset tangentially from obstacle midpoint; two-phase joint-space interpolation
  • Jacobian controller: goal attraction + per-link tangential avoidance; gains tuned (katt=1.5, kavoid=0.4)
  • Stochastic red-cube obstacle placement in Genesis physics engine
  • PPO RL framework explored for via-point learning; computational cost prevented convergence

Space Rover Platform
Ground-up rover with Ackermann steering and 50 Hz closed-loop control on RP2040. Solved 3–5 A motor current spikes through capacitive decoupling and isolated regulated power rails.
+
Overview

Ongoing rover project at Columbia University's Stark Lab, supervised by Prof. Evan Lerner. Building a complete mobile platform from scratch integrating mechanical, electrical, and control subsystems. Ackermann steering with DC motors and encoders, 50 Hz closed-loop control on RP2040. Motor current spikes (3–5 A) caused voltage drops and resets — resolved with 2200 µF decoupling capacitors, isolated power rails (11.1 V → 5 V for Pi / 6 V for servos), common ground architecture, and correctly sized wire gauges.

Technical Highlights
  • Ackermann steering, DC motors, encoders; 50 Hz closed-loop on RP2040
  • 3–5 A motor spikes solved: 2200 µF caps across motor driver VIN/GND
  • Isolated rails: 11.1 V → 5 V (Pi) / 6 V (servos); common ground; 5 A regulators

RL for Restaurant Inventory Management
PPO + DeepSets policy for perishable inventory management. 77% spoilage reduction vs. heuristic baselines in simulation. Collaboration with FoodWeb.ai.
+
Overview

Final project for Reinforcement Learning at Columbia University (Prof. Shipra Agrawal), in collaboration with FoodWeb.ai. Developed a PPO agent with DeepSets architecture for perishable inventory management across multiple restaurants under budget and capacity constraints. Policy integrated LSTM-based demand forecasts and LogNormal action distributions. Achieved 77% spoilage reduction vs. heuristic baselines in simulation.

Technical Highlights
  • PPO + DeepSets for permutation-invariant multi-ingredient, multi-restaurant state spaces
  • LSTM demand forecasts + LogNormal action distributions
  • 77% spoilage reduction and higher profit vs. heuristic baselines

Recycling Conveyor Sorting System
Vision-based recycling sorter integrating a 3D-printed conveyor, ultrasonic sensing, overhead camera, Raspberry Pi inference, and a rail-mounted bin system for three waste categories.
+
Overview

Final project for Industrial Automation at Columbia University (Prof. Ali Dadgar). Integrated a 3D-printed conveyor belt with a multi-stage sensing pipeline. An ultrasonic sensor triggered servo ejection of oversized objects; an overhead camera fed a trained vision model on Raspberry Pi to classify items into recyclable, compost, or non-recyclable categories. A rail-mounted bin system aligned outputs accordingly.

Technical Highlights
  • 3D-printed conveyor powered by DC motors; CAD-designed for repeatable transport
  • Ultrasonic sensing + servo ejection gate for oversized objects
  • Raspberry Pi running trained vision model for real-time three-class classification
  • Rail-mounted bin system: recyclable / compost / non-recyclable
